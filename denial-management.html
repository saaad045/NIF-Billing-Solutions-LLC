Understanding Denial Management

What is Denial Management?

Denial management is the process of identifying, analyzing, and appealing denied insurance claims to recover revenue and prevent future denials. It involves tracking denial reasons, correcting errors, resubmitting claims, and implementing preventive measures.

At NIF Billing Solutions LLC, our denial management experts use AI-powered analytics to identify denial patterns, correct root causes, and appeal denied claims aggressively. We help practices reduce denial rates and recover millions in lost revenue.

Denial Management FAQs

Everything you need to know about denial management

The average denial rate for medical claims is 5-10% for most practices. However, this varies by specialty and payer. Our denial management services typically reduce denial rates to under 3% within 6 months.

We use AI-powered denial tracking software that automatically captures denial data from payer remittance advices. Our system categorizes denials by reason, payer, provider, and procedure, providing real-time analytics and actionable insights to prevent future denials.

We offer flexible pricing models including percentage of recovered revenue, flat monthly fee, or per-appeal basis. Most clients see a 5-10x return on investment. Contact us for a customized quote based on your denial volume and practice size.

The appeals process varies by payer and denial reason, typically taking 30-90 days for resolution. Our team files appeals promptly and follows up aggressively to ensure timely resolution. We provide regular status updates throughout the process.

While not all denials can be successfully appealed, approximately 60-70% of denials are recoverable with proper documentation and timely filing. Our expert team analyzes each denial to determine the best course of action and achieves an 85% success rate on appealed claims.
